  • Big Movers of the Week
    • Ulta Beauty Inc (ULTA): 471.46 (17.00%)
      • Ulta shares skyrocketed Friday following better than expected reports with strong first quarter EPS coming in at $6.70 and an increase in net sales by 4.5% to $2.8 billion. Company remarked on lower inventory losses and new product launches driving demand as well as double digit growth in their fragrance category. Ulta operated 1,451 stores as of this quarter.
    • Hologic Inc (HOLX): 62.17 (15.04%)
      • HOLX shares jumped following news of PE firms Blackstone and TPG making offers to acquire the company. Hologic rejected the offers valuing the company at over $16 billion, a premium of $70–$72 per share.
    • Palantir Technologies (PLTR): 131.78 (9.07%)
      • Palantir continues growing, hitting a new 52-wk high as President Trump calls on the company to help compile data. Since Trump took office the company has received over $113 million in government spending and was recently awarded a $795 million contract from the DOD. Talks are arising concerning negotiations from other agencies about acquiring more technology, as rising demand for Palantir’s Foundry data analytics program rises.
    • Regeneron Pharmaceuticals (REGN): 490.28 (-16.46%)
      • Marking the stock's lowest level since 2021, Regeneron shares fell almost 19% Thursday-Friday as one of the company's partnered treatments yielded mixed results in a Phase 3 study. To get FDA approval Regeneron will likely have to re-run the Phase 3 study, with the stock price reflecting waning confidence in both investors and commercial partners.

U.S. Monetary & Fiscal Policy:

Dollar Dips: The U.S. dollar slumped to its lowest level since 2023, with the Dollar Index (DXY) hovering under 100.00 at 99.21 and Bloomberg’s Dollar Spot Index also sliding. The drop comes amid growing investor anxiety over new tariff threats and impending court decisions, a widening fiscal deficit, and signs of slowing GDP growth. As uncertainty builds, traders and asset managers continue to bet against the dollar, both domestically and overseas.

Global Macroeconomic News:

Tariff Talk: A federal appeals court has temporarily paused a recent ruling that blocked the imposition of global tariffs, arguing they exceeded executive authority. Still, the administration says they have other ways to implement duties as President Donald Trump is set to raise current levies on both steel and aluminum to 50%. While claiming the new tariff will benefit American workers and help a deal between Nippon Steel and US Steel, many businesses stay wary and prepare for price hikes.

Crude Intentions: OPEC decides to increase production to 411,000 barrels a day beginning in July, the third consecutive month increase. OPEC reaffirmed its commitment to market stability amid steady global fundamentals with this hike, a move that could pressure U.S. oil producers already squeezed by high drilling costs and declining prices.

PCE & Core PCE: US economy shows signs of slowing as Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) Index rose only 0.1% from last month's increase of 0.7%, while Core PCE excluding food and energy also only rose 0.1% monthly. Annual Core PCE remained the same compared to last year's increase of 2.5%. Marking the smallest increase in 4 years, inflationary concerns remain over potential price spikes and ongoing tariff tensions and trade negotiations.
