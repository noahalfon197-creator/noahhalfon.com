Crypto Catalysts

Regulation: Crypto regulations like the Genius Act are being signed into legislation, helping establish guardrails and consumer protections for stablecoin infrastructure. The bill aims to bring backing and compliance, allowing institutional legitimacy and a true green light to enter this space. These types of regulatory measures could be the key to unlocking trillions transactions, especially for global payments and treasury management. Basically, a stablecoin is a cryptocurrency tied to a stable asset like the US dollar. What this means is that the coin is able to maintain a consistent value for easy and reliable digital transactions. Stablecoin networks are already becoming a dominant force in cross border payments, where crypto allows for faster and cheaper transactions, where middlemen and service costs are bypassed by direct digital payments. We are already hearing of companies like Amazon and Walmart thinking of implementing their own stablecoins, allowing these merchant companies to bypass costly credit card networks and services. The market has responded well to these movements already, with companies like Circle Internet Group (CRCL is a company that provides infrastructure for stablecoin and blockchain applications) entering the public market with an IPO which rallied more than 800%. Further proving infrastructure confidence is at an all time high, CRCL is seeing their stablecoin supply skyrocket while the dollar has struggled this year.

Potentially Impacted Stocks: V, MA, PYPL

Backing & Blockchain: We are also seeing a strategic shift exclusively into ETH backed securities/treasuries as Ethereum represents the opportunity to better management systems. To better explain, instead of holding cash in traditional bank accounts, companies and protocols can hold ETH or tokenized assets (what we covered before, but think Treasury bills etc.) on a blockchain. The blockchain simply represents a digital notebook where all transactions are instantly recorded, but unlike traditional cash reserves, the blockchain offers a public, real time record. Blockchains are also global, work around the clock, and all you need to access one is a digital wallet and internet. This enables stakeholders/investors to monitor movements as they happen and with 24/7 transparency. As institutions pivot toward crypto backed structures, ETH is the go to for tokenized treasuries. A clear example of this trend is BitMine Immersion Technologies (BMNR). Under the leadership of newly appointed CEO Tom Lee, BMNR is allocating Ethereum as its primary treasury reserve asset, closely following MicroStrategy’s (MSTR) former Bitcoin play. By doing so, BMNR aims to not only hedge against the inflation and debasement we talked about before, but also position itself as a firm aligned with a future of finance. ETH backed reserves are not just a hedge, but actually are signs for institutional adoption and a signal that crypto may be turning into more of a commodity than we think.

Tokenization + Expansion: Tokenization is also rapidly transforming fintech by turning assets into digital tokens on the blockchain. The largest category of tokenized assets are those stablecoins, which we said are tokens pegged to the value of another asset, like currencies etc. Stablecoins are emerging as technology through which users and companies can transact faster and cheaper than the traditional payment system like banks or credit companies. Tokenization has allowed platforms like Robinhood (HOOD +315.79% YoY) to take this technology and offer retail investors access to asset classes that may not be available in their current location or available on their subsequent brokerages. By using blockchain technology, the same global decentralized infrastructure that powers cryptocurrencies, this invention has the potential to expand to assets once held only by private institutions. We are actively seeing the expansion of fintech, where a once small population of retail investors are now expanding rapidly, and institutions are actively backing these moves. If tokenization can become bigger, more brokerages figure out a system where they can implement this tech, I believe we are edging closer to democratizing finance.

Dollar Debasement & Inflation: As the BBB ramps up, concerns over added fiscal debt and a growing deficit continue. The logic is simple; when the government spends aggressively and adds trillions to the deficit, it increases the risk of dollar debasement, meaning the value of the dollar erodes over time due to inflation or continued money printing. In this environment, investors look to store value in assets that can’t be inflated or debased. Bitcoin specifically, along with a growing Ethereum fanbase, are global decentralized assets that are seen as a hedge against inflation, fitting this role perfectly as new highs continue.

Takeaways:

You definitely don’t need to be directly involved in crypto to benefit from this. There are numerous companies pioneering crypto and AI together, as well as companies building different applications using blockchain technology. Companies leading this convergence or taking a different approach could be a real opportunity in the longer run, but one breakthrough or interesting invention could send a company's stock flying in days. On another hand, as computing power and AI models continue to grow, I believe a real opportunity lies in energy. As AI workloads and online payments increase, the US power supply and energy grid could face real problems. Companies that have solutions for energy struggles or companies that are developing new technology to handle that increased stress may be an interesting look.

U.S. Monetary & Fiscal Policy

CME Fedwatch: As positive economic data this week has shown inflation coming in milder than expected, President Trump and other members from within the Fed have stepped up calls to cut rates before the labor market weakens. For the July Fed meeting, predictions show only a 4.7% chance of a cut. While many of the larger Spring tariff threats have been downsized or delayed to the new deadline of August 1st, Fed Chair Powell has remained in a resilient wait and see mode, but did outline that continued milder than expected readings could warrant cuts by the end of summer. Moving forward to the September meeting, probabilities of a 25 basis point cut have risen to 56.1%, but the decision hinges heavily on ongoing tariff related inflation. So far, many companies have been able to shield consumers from increased prices, resulting from frontloading goods and absorbing costs, but that could change as we move into the historically slower late summer period.

TACO Trades: TACO, referring to the acronym “Trump always chickens out” appears to be the consensus as more tariff threats emerge as a presuming negotiation tactic. This week, President Trump said the US will impose a 35% tariff on Canadian imports, 50% levy on Brazilian goods, and is currently pushing for a 15-20% minimum tariff on all goods from the EU. Additionally, letters will be sent to smaller countries setting tariffs of 10% or higher. With a firm deadline of August 1st, many nations are now rushing to negotiate deals. Commerce Secretary Lutnick emphasized, “The larger economies will either open their markets or pay a fair tariff to America.”

Global Macroeconomic News

CPI and Core CPI: CPI for the month of June rises 0.3% to 2.7% YoY, but is in line with expectations. Core CPI excluding food and energy rose 0.2% to come in line with expectations of 2.9%. . The shelter index rose 0.2% in June and is the main reason items increased, while goods exposed to tariffs (toys and appliances) rose by the fastest pace in years. For Core, vehicles both used and new as well as airline fares all decreased. While actual inflation still remains well above the Fed's 2% target, markets remained green as a result of this data.

PPI: Producer Price index comes in amazingly well at 2.3% YoY VS the 2.6% expected. Core PPI, excluding volatile food and energy also came in at 2.6%, well below expectations of 2.8%. Both month over month PPI and Core PPI came in at a 0% increase under a 0.3% estimate. This data reflects inflation continuing to come down, but concern remains over both new and old tariffs threatening inflation and costs being passed to the end consumer in the coming months.
