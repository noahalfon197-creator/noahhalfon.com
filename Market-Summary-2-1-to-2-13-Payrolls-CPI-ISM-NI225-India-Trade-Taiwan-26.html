Global Macroeconomic News

ISM: January ISM Manufacturing PMI surged to 52.6, up 4.7 points from December's 47.9 and beating the 48.5 consensus. While marking the first expansion in 12 months, employment disappointed at 50.3 vs 51.8 expected, suggesting firms remain cautious on hiring, while prices paid also rose, reinforcing cost pressures. This data release helped push equities higher last Monday and reinforced some treasury selloffs.

CPI: January CPI comes in at 2.4% YoY vs expectations of 2.5%. Core CPI excluding volatile food and energy comes in line at 2.5%. The slight downside surprise on headline inflation helps calm short term rate concerns while reinforcing a Fed wait and see.

SaaSpocalypse: Software continues to get destroyed as Anthropic automation tools heighten concerns over software's core business. After releasing a new automation tool for law firms, investors continue to sell all software even though many top firms are doing well. Continued selling has also bled into credit markets, where billions of dollars of loans dropped to distressed levels.

India: US and India reach a trade deal with the US, lowering tariffs on Indian goods from 25-18%. This deal helps reduce overall levies from earlier in the year from 50%-18%. In return, India has pledged to purchase $500 billion in US energy, technology, agricultural products, coal, and aircraft over the next five years, and reduce its purchasing of Russian oil. The alpha from these deals are the companies who ate tariff costs and have compressed margins over the last six months, and have meaningful US revenue exposure.

Nonfarm Payrolls/Unemployment: Nonfarm payrolls come in huge at +130,000 vs the 70,000 expected. The unemployment rate also dropped to 4.3% from 4.4% in December. This data helped boost markets and treasury yields as well as cut hopes for a June rate cut to under 50% as the labor market continues to appear stable. I still see only one to no cuts this year as the Fed remains in a wait and see despite the administration's calls.

Taiwan: US and Taiwan sign trade agreement to cut US tariffs on Taiwanese exports to 15%. In exchange, Taiwan will remove/reduce 99% of US tariff barriers and pledge $84B in US goods like energy and aviation. In other Taiwanese news, Taiwan ups its 2026 growth forecast from 3.54% to 7.71% citing AI export demand. Taiwan January exports came in at +69.9% YoY (fastest in 16 years), and the finance ministry looking for Feb exports +20% to +27% YoY.

ECB: The European Central Bank decides to keep interest rates unchanged at 2.0%, signaling a cautious stance against inflation and growth concerns. Policymakers emphasized data dependence, noting progress on core inflation but lasting wage pressures and geopolitical risks. EU Markets interpreted the tone as mildly dovish, as yields edged lower and rate-cut expectations for later this year decreases. Eyes on incoming labor and inflation data continue, similar to the US.
