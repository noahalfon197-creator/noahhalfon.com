Big Movers of the Week

    • Microchip Technologies (MCHP): 65.81 (24.45%)
      • Microchip saw shares rise after raising Q4 guidance. Increasing expectations to the high end of their guidance to reflect 12% YoY revenue growth citing increased backlog and bookings. MCHP’s bull case still lies in a “multi year recovery” investment thesis, which is based on a rebound in industrial and automotive demand.
    • Dollar General Corp (DG): 132.37 (21.27%)
      • DG reports Q3 earnings upping guidance and claiming gains in every category they report in. EPS came in at $1.28 vs $0.95 expected, up 40% YoY, while revenue grew 4.6%. Inflation worries and consumer confidence seems to be pushing sales as same store sales are up 2.5%, while the company reports that 47,000 real estate projects with 450 new store openings will be coming in the next year.
    • Paramount Skydance Corp (PSKY): 13.37 (-15.28%)
      • PSKY falls on news of losing to Netflix in a billion-dollar bidding war to acquire Warner Bros Discovery. PSKY was reportedly seeking this acquisition to accelerate its growth and better compete in the streaming wars. The failure to secure the deal raises questions about the company's future growth path and its ability to achieve scale against competitors.

U.S. Monetary & Fiscal Policy

End of QT: US Federal Reserve formally announces the end of their quantitative tightening to address mounting stress and volatility in short term funding markets. The end of QT may shift investor appetite towards risk, and with rate cuts expected soon, monetary conditions look set to ease further. President Trump is also reportedly preparing to announce the next Fed chair, a move that could diminish Powell’s influence during a highly sensitive macro period. (see top graph)

Global Macroeconomic News

Private Payrolls: US ADP Private payrolls come in at -32,000 vs the 40,000 expected. With unemployment sitting at a high of 4.4%, this is four months out of six where payrolls have declined. Companies with fewer than 50 employees shed 120,000 jobs, the largest one month decline since May 2020.

PCE and Core PCE: Headline PCE comes in at 2.8% increasing 0.3%, while Core PCE, excluding volatile food and energy, rose 0.2% while the annual rate stays at 2.8%. The release stated that personal income rose 0.4% on the month while spending was up 0.3%, with food up 0.4% and energy up 1.7%. This report helps strengthen the argument that inflation is under control and that rate cuts are needed to support a weakening labor market.

India Central Bank: India’s central bank delivered its first rate cut in half a year, with the RBI’s policy committee voting unanimously to lower the repurchase rate by 25 bps to 5.25% as policymakers look to cushion growth against pressure from elevated U.S. tariffs. Alongside the cut, the RBI announced plans to inject additional liquidity into the system, committing to purchase 1 trillion rupees ($11B) in government bonds this month.

M&A

Netflix Warner: Netflix (NFLX) outbids Paramount Skydance to acquire Warner Bros. Discovery (WBD) for $72 billion. The deal values WBD at $27.75 per share, representing an $82.7B enterprise value. This deal follows WBD’s plan to split its businesses into two separate public companies, separating its’ traditional cable networks and channels. Both boards unanimously approved the transaction, with closing predicted in 12-18 months pending the completion of the spin off and a WBD shareholder vote. From this merger, Netflix will gain deep studio level production capacity and a legacy catalog from this deal, but many in Hollywood are remarking concerns over growing industry consolidation and potential erosion of creative diversity.
