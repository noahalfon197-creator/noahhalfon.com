U.S. Monetary & Fiscal Policy:

US China Talks: US and China wrapped up trade talks in London, agreeing on a framework to speed up rare-earth shipments that are critical to US manufacturers, while the US eases up on tech export controls. While awaiting approval from both leaders, markets barely reacted, reflecting skepticism about the deal’s depth. However this is a positive sign that both sides are willing to engage.

Global Macroeconomic News:

CPI and Core CPI: May inflation data comes in softer than expected with CPI rising 0.1% to 2.4, vs the expected 2.5. Core CPI, minus food and energy stays at 2.8% for the third month in a row at its lowest level since 2021. While easing inflation data has offered some relief for consumers, the FED is continuously pressured to cut rates while businesses continue to face margin pressure. Sustained oil prices could complicate the outlook as we head later into June.

PPI and Core PPI: Both PPI and Core PPI excluding food and energy rose by 0.1%. This report follows the tamer CPI data, showing less reaction and muted inflation in response to tariffs than many thought. As this data comes out, traders bet on 2 FED rate cuts by year-end.

Consumer Sentiment: The University of Michigan’s early June consumer survey showed a surprising jump in consumer sentiment, with the index rising to 60.5, well above expectations of 54. Consumers appear to be recovering from the initial shock of recent tariff escalations, helped by a cooling inflation outlook. In light of this reinforcing data, one year inflation expectations dropped to 5.1%, the lowest since 1981. While this news is great for consumers, President Trump and the White House are quick to demand rate cuts by the FED as we await next week's FOMC meeting.
