U.S. Monetary & Fiscal Policy

Jobless Claims: Continuing jobless claims climbed to their highest level since November 2021, along with initial claims coming in at +226K vs the expected +221K. Among this report we can see the labor market is slowly cooling, with President Trump announcing that he would fire BLS commissioner Dr. Erika McEntarfer for swaying recent numbers. Markets actually reacted modestly positively to this news, as weak labor market data and rising claims only strengthened the argument for rate cuts and more liquidity injected into the market.

CME Fedwatch: With the labor market starting to show cracks in the economy, calls for a rate cut have been strengthened proportionally. September probabilities for a 25 basis point cut to 4.0-4.25% have risen to above 80.0%. Strengthened by weak labor data and continued calls from a growing side of dissenters, Federal Reserve Governor Michelle Bowman spoke out this week citing that waiting too long to cut could have adverse effects on slowing the US economy and letting the labor market weaken even more. We wait for next week's CPI data to see more changes.

Positioning With a Rate Cut: When rates come down borrowing costs likewise decrease, promoting a high growth high leverage environment. Having high leverage (meaning high debt to equity ratio) can create a bigger advantage when rates come down, meaning most companies won't have to borrow tons of new money and can instead refinance existing loans. With this strategy, many small cap companies or earlier stage companies with lots of growth potential and high levels of debt may benefit tremendously. Even with a declining FED balance sheet, lower rates mean you don’t have to worry about a company going bankrupt if they are making no profits.

Under such an environment money is relatively "cheap," meaning a company can take on more debt than before to survive. Depending on total market liquidity, (if high) we can look to high growth companies and and then find the capturable inefficiency to invest, or (if lower) we can look towards companies with strong profitability and high margins that may be better positioned. In my view, even with a tightening balance sheet, AI tailwinds and strong earnings are sustaining growth and continuing to push liquidity into the market.

Global Macroeconomic News

Stagflation: Signs of stagflation appear to be resurfacing as trade and inflation data flash warning signals. June trade deficit figures show a drop in U.S. imports, reversing previous gains from companies stockpiling goods ahead of tariffs. June trade data for Canada also widened to second largest ever, with Canada's exports to the US dropping 12.5% when compared with the same period a year ago. U.S. services ISM data also showed the highest price pressures in the service sector since 2022, signaling that businesses are facing rising costs while growth appears to be slowing altogether. President Trump’s continued new tariffs could slow growth and keep inflation sticky, complicating the Fed’s rate cut path past September. While markets are currently pricing in September cuts, risks to inflation remain as tariff rates may be passed to the end consumer.

Banking AI & Upside Case: Financial service companies are making the most of AI as major banks continue to expand and implement AI. This week, Wells Fargo expanded an ongoing partnership with Google Cloud to use AI agents throughout all workforce levels including AI for call centers or corporate banking. We also have seen AI being used for deal material review and compliance, with other firms like J.P. Morgan already has 200K employees partnered with Amazon Work Services (AWS).

As we head into the historical slower later summer months, the financial sector poses an interesting investment that has increased upside potential. With AI becoming a more widespread application and finally being institutionalized, financials represent a fundamental case of change. We all know AI is capable of automating tasks and is already helping firms develop new products, but by helping to sift through large amounts of data, AI allows said services to focus more on personalization and find security/fraud risks at much faster rates. By speeding up processes and cutting out formerly labor intensive roles, AI will allow for margin expansion, either in earnings growth or passing cost savings onto consumers for more affordable services. Given the financial sector’s current lower multiples, these improvements could lead to higher valuations as the market price in the potential for stronger future performance. Moreover, with the current administration focusing on domestic economic growth and heavily pushing for rate cuts, banks are likely to benefit from improved yield opportunities. Lower rates typically increase loan demand and propel deal activity, whether that be IPO, M&A, or refinancing, the financial sector will surely see profits and better market valuation.
